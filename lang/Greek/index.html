Σπουδαίος! - Important!

This is a page about Modern Greek pronounciation, really it doesn't matter what kind of Greek pronounciation you use for Koine Greek (Ελληνιστική Κοινή), but for Modern Greek it matters. It doesn't matter if you use Modern pronounciation for Koine texts, just like how you don't need to know Shakespearean phonology to read Shakespearean English, although some things would rhyme better if you knew Shakespearean phonology but it's not something you need if that makes sense. Anyways, here is a list of pronounciations:

Modern Greek Reconstructed New Testament Roman-Palestinian Greek Erasmian (common in America for some reason) Classical (Attic) Greek Fraternity, Physics, and Calculus

Is it a difficult script to learn?

The Greek alphabet is really easy to learn. The roman alphabet (the one you're reading right now) is based upon the Greek alphabet, so you'll find many characters that are the same or similar across both alphabets. In fact, the English word "alphabet" comes from the first to letters of the Greek one (Alpha + beta = alphabet).

Vowles
ΑΙ αι ai [e] E sound *
ει, οι, υι ei, oi, yi [i] i sound like ήτα
ΟΥ ου oü [u] U sound, like in Loot
ΑΥ αυ aü [av, af] either av or af
ΕΥ ευ eü [ev, ef] either ev or ef

* αι is either e or like the English word "yeah", so in the word "είναι" is pronounced "ine", but "και" is pronounced "kyeah", or like "kje". So it's pronounced either like a latin "e" or like a cyrillic "е".

** τσ & τζ are also two letter consonants, I've not included them above since they're pronounced like they look. Like ts as one sound, like in its.

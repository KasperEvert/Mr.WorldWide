Zoroastrianism?

The Avestan language is primarialy used for religius scripture of the Zoroastrian religion. The religion is named after 𐬰𐬀𐬭𐬀𐬚𐬎𐬱𐬙𐬭𐬀 - Zaraθuštra, who is to Zoroastrianism like Jesus is to Christianity.

Other Zoroastrian concepts, some of which you might even have heard:

𐬁𐬙𐬀𐬭 - ātar, is the Zoroastrian concept of a "holy" fire (the word holy there is pretty vague, it's not like Zoroastrians treat fire like a good or anything).

𐬀𐬴𐬀 - aṣ̌a, is the concept of correctness or right(eousness). You might have for example heard of the song "Brimful of Asha", you have the concept right there in the title.

𐬛𐬭𐬎𐬘 - druj, is the opposite of aṣ̌a, and means deceit or falsehood.

𐬫𐬀𐬰𐬀𐬙𐬀 - yazata, is the Zoroastrian concept of divinity. It is usually defined along the lines of worthy of worship or worthy of veneration.

𐬨𐬀𐬰𐬛𐬁 𐬀𐬵𐬎𐬭𐬀 - Mazdā Ahura, the creator of the universe, a god in Zoroastrian belief.

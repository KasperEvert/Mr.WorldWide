Transliteration of "Frösöstenen/Frøsøysteina"

AustmaÞr GuÞfastar Sun lit rai(sa stein) Þina äuk irua bru Þis auk han lit kristna Iatalant Asbiurn kirÞi bru Triun raist äuk Tsain runar Þisar

Translation of "Frösöstenen/Frøsøysteina"

Modern Swedish literal translation

Austmthr Guthfastar Sun lät resa denna sten och göra denna bro och han lät kristna Iatalant Asbiurn gjorde bron Triun ristade och Tsain dessa runor.

Modern Swedish translation

Östman Gudfast Son lät resa denna sten, göra denna bro och han lät kristna Jämtland. Åsbjörn gjorde bron. Tryn och Sten ristade dessa runor.

English translation

Austmthr Guthfastar Sun (The man from the east, son of the godfearing one) let raise this stone, make this bridge and he let Jamtland become Christian. Asbiurn (The asa bear) built the bridge. Triun and Tsain (Peter) carved these runes.

Notes

In this text we see a couple of interesting things about how it is written. Firstly we have three runes which aren't too common among Swedish rune stones. We also have an interesting word for "Jämtland". So the name "Jämtland" has three versions. The Jamtish/Old-Norwegian "Jamtland/Jamtaland/Jamtlänn", the Danish "Jemtland" and the Swedish "Jämtland". Here we see it spelled like Iatalant, the "i" here representing the same sound like an english "y", and so too does the "j" in the modern spellings. This was probably pronounced with an "m". In a lot of rune stones the letters "n" and "m" were not written. It is probably supposed to be pronounced "Jamtalant".

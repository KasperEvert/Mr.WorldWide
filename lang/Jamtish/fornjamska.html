Transliteration of "Frösöstenen"

AustmaÞr GuÞfastaR Sun lit rai(sa stein) Þina æuk irua bru Þis auk han lit kristna Iatalant Asbiurn kirÞi bru Triun raist æuk Tsain runaR ÞisaR

Transliteration with corrections

AustmaÞr GuÞfastaR son lit reisa stein Þina æuk girua bru Þis auk han lit kristna Jamtaland Asbiurn girÞi bru Triun æuk Stein reist runaR ÞisaR

Translation of "Frösöstenen"

Modern Swedish translation

Östman Gudfastes Son lät resa denna sten, göra denna bro och han lät kristna Jämtland. Åsbjörn gjorde bron. Tryn och Sten ristade dessa runor.

English translation

Austmthr Guthfastar Sun (The man from the east, son of the godfearing one) let raise this stone, make this bridge and he let Christianize Jamtland. Asbiurn (The "asa" bear) built the bridge. Triun and Stein (Peter) carved these runes.

Notes

In this text we see a couple of interesting things about how it is written. Firstly we have three runes which aren't too common among Swedish rune stones. We also have an interesting word for "Jämtland". So the name "Jämtland" has three versions. The Jamtish/Old-Norwegian "Jamtland/Jamtaland/Jamtlänn", the Danish "Jemtland" and the Swedish version of the danish name "Jämtland". Here we see it spelled like Iatalant, the "i" here is used as a consonant, representing the same sound like an english "y", and so too does the "j" in the modern spellings. This was probably pronounced with an "m". In a lot of rune stones, nasal consonants, the letters "n" and "m", were not written. It is probably supposed to be pronounced "Jamtaland".

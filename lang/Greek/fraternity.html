Σπουδαίος! - Important!

This is a page about Fraternity Greek pronounciation, This is in reference to just invalid englishified Greek. It's like a mix of Classical Attic and Erasmian but more English. This is the way your physics teacher spoke Greek, and he learned this pronunciation in his fraternity. Next time you hear a physics teacher pronounce Greek, laugh and look superior. Anyways, here is a list of pronounciations:

Modern Greek Reconstructed New Testament Roman-Palestinian Greek Erasmian (common in America for some reason) Classical (Attic) Greek Fraternity, Physics, and Calculus

The alphabet

Note: Sigma has a diffrent character when at the end of the word. That character is marked with "final".

Note 2: When I say "i", I usually mean a non-english, but not here.

Note 3: When it says "ä", that's the letter ash, which makes the same sound as the a in "ash", an actual greek a it kinda depends but its usually one of the "a":s in "mama".

Note 4: All two-letter vowels and consonants have been removed since no teacher cares about that.

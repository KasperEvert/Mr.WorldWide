PIE

Proto-Indo-European

Important!

This page aims to give some sort of basic way to read PIE reconstructions by giving example of how some reconstruct PIE pronounciation, using IPA. This is not a page that should be used as a source for actual attempts at reconstructing PIE pronounciation.

Vowels

As you might be able to understand, vowels are very difficult to reconstruct in proto languages. For the most part we can tell short and long vowels apart due to many of the decendants of PIE having had at least two vowel qualities. Usually either the more "Old-Norse" kind of letters are used: i = /i/, í = /iː/. Or Latin letters: i = /i/, ī = /iː/. We can also usually tell if a vowel was round or not. Apart from that it doesn't really matter how you read vowels when reading PIE in your head. Y is used as a consonant, specifically for a /j/ sound. Apart from that, usually the standard Latin vowels are used. In other words: AEIOU.

Special characters

The circle under a consonant, the one that is used for voiceless consonants in IPA, is used for syllabic consonants in PIE. So r̥ = /r̩/. "ʷ" represents labialization, just like in IPA. so kʷ = /kʷ/. "ʰ" represents a "breathy voiced" sound. So gʰ = /ɡʱ/.

Is it a difficult script to learn?

The hebrew alphabet is sometimes treated as much harder than it actually is. So depending on which sound you think the letter "י - Yod" that makes a y/i/j sound which might at first sound quite difficult to know which to use, but in reality the J & Y both represent the same sound, like the J in Julia, and so you could make it even easier to learn.

So in other words the character "Yod" makes either a y/i/e sound. "i" is similar to both so for most of the the pronounciation will come naturally. So don't worry about the two to four almost identical or in some cases (like with j/y) exactly identical sounds.

What does "Alef beit" mean?

So "Alef beit" is just the name of the first two letters of the alphabet. Just like our word in english "Alphabet" is just the name of the first to letters of the Greek alphabet (which the roman one is based upon): Alpha Beta.
